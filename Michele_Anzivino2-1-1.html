Oral History Presentation

Michele Anzivino

A life in hairdressing, built on craft, discipline, and the belief that a stylist should be a daymaker, not just a hairdresser.

Presentation by Alexander Anzivino

Who Michele Is

Birthplace

Italy

Michele was born in Italy before spending most of his childhood in England.

Raised In

Bedford, England

He described growing up there as “fantastic,” full of freedom, soccer, and neighborhood life.

Career Start

Since 1978

He has worked in hairdressing for decades and now lives in Gainesville, Florida.

A Journey Into Hairdressing

Turning Point

“My mother told me that I should learn a trade.”

That advice pushed him toward a profession he already admired because the hairdressers he knew seemed creative and confident.

Moving To Miami

At 18, Michele moved from England to Miami to begin his career. He remembered the change vividly: England felt cold and black-and-white, while Miami felt bright, colorful, and alive.

18

Age when he moved to Miami.

1978

Year he says his hair career began.

More Than A Salon

First Impressions

  • Michele’s early salon memories center on atmosphere and energy.
  • He remembered “great vibes” that made the salon feel welcoming.
  • Clients came in to relax and enjoy a couple of hours, not only to get a haircut.

What That Means

  • From the start, he saw beauty work as an experience.
  • The salon became a space for comfort, trust, and conversation.
  • That idea later becomes central to his “daymaker” philosophy.

The Craft And The Training

🎓

Serious Training

Michele believes hairdressing is a skilled craft that demands years of discipline, not quick shortcuts.

✂️

Vidal Sassoon

He calls Vidal Sassoon “the fundamental of all haircuts” and credits that tradition with shaping modern styles.

📚

Advice

His advice to new stylists is simple: train hard, find the best academy for you, and never stop learning.

How The Industry Changed

Then vs. Now

Then: work spread by word of mouth, magazines, and people seeing your cuts in real life.

Now: Instagram can make “everybody a famous hairdresser,” changing how stylists build reputation.

Effect: the job is more visible now, but also shaped by edited images and faster expectations.

Michele’s View

“In the past, it was all word of mouth. Your work spoke in volumes.”

He sees social media as a powerful tool, but he also suggests it can make skill look easier or more glamorous than it really is.

Client Expectations And Honesty

Edited Images

Michele says some looks clients see online are heavily edited or photoshopped, which creates unrealistic expectations.

Hair Reality

A hairstyle depends on real factors like texture, volume, and what a client’s hair can actually do.

Professional Ethics

For him, honesty matters as much as talent. A stylist has to tell the truth about what is possible.

What The Job Demands

🧠

Mental

He says the job is mentally demanding because you must stay focused and creative all day.

💬

Social

Stylists often need to maintain conversation and connection even when they are tired.

💪

Physical

Standing for long hours means the work also takes a real physical toll on the body.

🎨

Creative

Each client is different, so technique and artistry have to work together in real time.

Core Theme

The Daymaker Philosophy

“You are a daymaker and not just a hairdresser.”

Michele believes the real job is not only cutting or coloring hair. It is treating people with respect, honesty, and kindness, and changing how they feel while they are in the chair.

Why That Philosophy Matters

What He Learned

  • You never know what people are going through.
  • Everyone has a different personality and brings a different emotional state into the salon.
  • A stylist has to put personal stress aside and give full attention to the client.

What Clients Need

  • Respect and kindness.
  • Honesty about what is realistic.
  • A feeling of care, comfort, and professionalism.

A Moment He Never Forgot

Before Christmas

A client came in seeming deeply upset, and Michele sensed something was wrong.

During The Appointment

He focused on giving her a great haircut and said her whole personality changed during the visit.

The Next Day

She emailed to thank him and said she had been planning to commit suicide that night, but something about the experience changed her mind.

Why It Matters

This story captures why Michele sees hairdressing as emotionally meaningful work, not just a service job.

What Kept Him Going

❤️

Love Of Hair

He says the craft itself still matters to him after decades in the profession.

🤝

Love Of People

Meeting clients and helping them leave happier gave him long-term motivation.

✨

Love Of Creativity

Creativity, positive salon energy, and human connection are what sustained his career.

Advice For Future Hairdressers

His Message

Train. Never stop learning. Go into the industry with love and passion.

Skill

Mastery takes years of practice, not just school hours.

Discipline

Find strong teachers and commit to fundamentals.

Heart

Real success depends on care, not only technical ability.

Michele’s Lasting Impact

Michele Anzivino’s interview shows that hairdressing is hard work, but it is also about caring for people. What matters most is not just the haircut itself, but how you make someone feel while they’re in your chair.
